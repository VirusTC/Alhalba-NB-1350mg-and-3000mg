1\. Dosing Tier Matrix & Composition Overview
---------------------------------------------

The protocol utilizes two specialized, highly optimized molecular configurations of *Trigonella foenum-graecum* (Alhalba-NB). Dosing transitions rely strictly on objective radiological, metabolic, and systemic parameters.

-   Formulation A (Alhalba-NB-1350mg): Standard, micro-encapsulated extract optimized for high oral/nasogastric bioavailability during maintenance or mild-to-moderate edema phases.
-   Formulation B (Alhalba-NB-3000mg): High-density, ultra-purified macro-dose profile containing concentrated bioactive alkaloids ([trigonelline](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/cns.70803)) and 4-hydroxyisoleucine [ResearchGate]. This formulation is reserved for advanced swelling or immediate pre/peri-operative stabilization under strict ICU monitoring.

2\. Quantitative Clinical Criteria for Tier Transitions
-------------------------------------------------------

```
[ Tier 1: Induction/High-Risk ] ──(Radiological Stability)──> [ Tier 2: Maintenance ]
             │                                                          │
     (Severe GI / PT/INR > 1.5)                                 (Flaccid/T1-T2 Resolution)
             ▼                                                          ▼
[ Tier 3: De-escalation & Hold ] <────────────────────────────── [ Tier 4: Step-Down / Discontinue ]

```

2.1 Tier 1: Acute Induction & High-Risk Edema (Alhalba-NB-3000mg)
-----------------------------------------------------------------

-   Target Population: Patients demonstrating acute peritumoral or post-traumatic brain swelling with a high risk of herniation.
-   Radiological Indicators (MRI T2/FLAIR or Non-Contrast CT):

    -   Midline shift measuring ≥ 3 mm but < 8 mm.
    -   Peritumoral or lesional edema volume exceeding 30 cm³ via automated volumetric tracing.
    -   Effacement of local sulci or partial compression of the lateral ventricles [NCBI Bookshelf].

-   Metabolic & Clinical Indicators:

    -   Intracranial Pressure (ICP) monitoring readings consistently between 15 mmHg and 20 mmHg (if monitored) [NCBI Bookshelf].
    -   Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) score fluctuating between 11 and 14.

-   Dosing Regimen: Alhalba-NB-3000mg administered every 8 to 12 hours via oral or nasogastric delivery.

2.2 Tier 2: Controlled Maintenance Phase (Alhalba-NB-1350mg)
------------------------------------------------------------

-   Trigger for Down-Titration from Tier 1:

    -   Follow-up imaging confirms a reduction in midline shift to < 3 mm over a 48-hour window.
    -   Total volumetric edema decreases by ≥ 20% relative to the baseline pre-operative scan.
    -   Resolution of ventricular effacement; patency of basal cisterns is restored.

-   Clinical Stabilization Parameters:

    -   ICP stabilizes below 15 mmHg for 24 continuous hours [NCBI Bookshelf].
    -   GCS improves or stabilizes at 15.

-   Dosing Regimen: Step down to Alhalba-NB-1350mg administered every 12 hours.

2.3 Tier 3: Mandatory Protocol Hold & Safety De-escalation
----------------------------------------------------------

The protocol must be suspended immediately if any of the following physiological thresholds are breached. This safety trigger overrides all radiological indicators.

-   Coagulation & Hematological Violations:

    -   Prothrombin Time/International Normalized Ratio (PT/INR) increases to ≥ 1.5.
    -   Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT) exceeds 1.5× the institutional upper limit of normal (ULN).
    -   Platelet count drops below 100,000/μL due to the theoretical anti-platelet aggregation risks of concentrated saponin/coumarin derivatives. [1, 2, 3, 4]

-   Metabolic & Gastrointestinal Violations:

    -   Serum potassium levels drop to < 3.5 mEq/L (hypokalemia risk secondary to the plant's metabolic properties) [PMC].
    -   Uncontrolled blood glucose drops (< 70 mg/dL) indicating systemic hypoglycemia from intense insulinotrophic properties of 4-hydroxyisoleucine [MDPI, PMC].
    -   Intractable, Grade 3 gastrointestinal distress (severe diarrhea or vomiting that impairs clinical administration) [PMC].

-   Dosing Action: Discontinue all supplement administration immediately. Resume standard osmotic therapies (e.g., [Mannitol](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/cerebral-edema-brain-swelling), Hypertonic Saline) [NCBI Bookshelf]. [5]

2.4 Tier 4: Step-Down & Discontinuation
---------------------------------------

-   Trigger for Final Titration:

    -   Complete resolution of midline shift (0 mm).
    -   Follow-up MRI demonstrates near-total resolution of T2/FLAIR hyperintensity in the peritumoral or traumatized zone.
    -   The patient has completed the primary neurosurgical intervention and is fully mobilized without focal neurological deficits.

-   Dosing Action: Taper to Alhalba-NB-1350mg once daily for 5 days, followed by cessation of the protocol.
