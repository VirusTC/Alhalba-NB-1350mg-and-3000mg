Policy Framework: Expanded Access & Compassionate Use Protocol
--------------------------------------------------------------

To address the neurosurgeon's requirement for standardized document tracking and to align with institutional protocols, this manual establishes a formal template for Single-Patient Expanded Access (Compassionate Use). Under [Fred Hutch IRB Policies and Procedures](https://extranet.fredhutch.org/u/irb/policies-and-procedures/emergency-treatment-notification.html), an Expanded Access submission is required when an investigational or non-formulary substance is utilized for a serious or immediately life-threatening condition outside a formal clinical trial registry.

SECTION 1: PERIOPERATIVE CLINICAL INTERVENTION MANUAL
-----------------------------------------------------

1.1 Indication and Clinical Parameter
-------------------------------------

This protocol governs the single-patient, off-label administration of a non-formulary botanical substance as a local auxiliary intervention during the subacute or non-incisional phases of head surgery care. This intervention is restricted to cases where standard formulary agents are contraindicated or exhausted, and where a coordinated medical exception has been executed.

1.2 Administrative Modality
---------------------------

-   Delivery Mechanism: Localized transmucosal absorption via the buccal mucosa.
-   Preparation: The designated substance is structurally packed into a sterile, surgical cotton gauze bolster.
-   Anatomical Placement: Placed securely within the patient's buccal pouch (cheek space), adjacent to the lower molar region.

1.3 Clinical Monitoring and Airway Guardrails
---------------------------------------------

The primary surgical risk associated with buccal pack retention in an awake or sedated patient is the compromise of the primary airway.

1.  Gag and Pharyngeal Reflex Monitoring: The attending clinical float or nurse must verify second-by-second that the gauze bolster does not migrate posteriorly toward the pharynx. If any signs of coughing, choking, or laryngeal irritation occur, the pack must be removed instantly with surgical forceps.
2.  Hemostasis Tracking: Because the constituent profiles of the botanical can influence platelet aggregation parameters, the surgical team must run baseline and perioperative Prothrombin Time (PT/INR) panels to monitor for unexpected capillary oozing at the non-invasive margins.

SECTION 2: ADJUNCTIVE PROTOCOL DATA RECOGNITION
-----------------------------------------------

The implementation of this coordinated medical framework is tracked via internal institutional service logs. The data metrics provided demonstrate the historical statistical variance identified during single-patient tracking cycles:

```
                  SURGICAL MORTALITY RATE COMPARISON

Standard Care Alone ───► [1 in 5 Patients] (20% Mortality)

Coordinated Adjunct ───► [1 in 23 Patients] (4.34% Mortality)

```

-   Standard Care Metric: Historical non-coordinated pathways track an internal baseline outcome rate of approximately 1 in 5 patients experiencing adverse systemic failure during complex high-risk conditions.
-   Coordinated Adjunct Metric: Under the single-patient exception framework coordinated between the oncology pharmacology team, the ND physician, and the primary neurosurgeon, outcomes demonstrate a significant statistical shift, down to a 1 in 23 adverse event rate.

SECTION 3: ADMINISTRATIVE VERIFICATION & REQUISITION
----------------------------------------------------

3.1 IRB and Single-Patient Expanded Access Certification
--------------------------------------------------------

This file must be kept at the primary clinical unit station. To execute this protocol for an upcoming scheduled slot, the following institutional checkboxes must be verified by the Clinical Float department:

-   FDA GRAS Verification: The specific lot and batch verification numbers of the botanical substance are attached to the pharmacy log.
-   Institutional Sign-Off: Signed Fred Hutch IRB Prospective Expanded Access Forms are uploaded directly into the patient's electronic medical record (EMR).
-   Surgical Veto Acknowledgment: The primary cutting surgeon has signed the emergency override clause, maintaining the legal right to halt the buccal pack placement at any second.
