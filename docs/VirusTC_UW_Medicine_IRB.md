Policy Framework: UW Medicine Institutional Single-Patient Expanded Access
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

To comply with the administrative mandates of UW Medicine (including UW Medical Center -- Montlake and UW Medical Center -- Northwest), this operational manual defines the protocol for a Single-Patient Expanded Access (Compassionate Use) pathway. Under standard [UW Medicine Human Subjects Division (HSD) Policies](https://www.washington.edu/research/hsd/), any clinical use of an investigational, non-formulary, or botanical substance for a serious or life-threatening condition outside an active clinical trial must be formally routed, tracked, and approved as an emergency or expanded access exception.

SECTION 1: UW MEDICINE PERIOPERATIVE CLINICAL INTERVENTION MANUAL
-----------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Indication and Institutional Parameters
-------------------------------------------

This protocol governs the single-patient, off-label administration of a non-formulary botanical substance as a local auxiliary intervention during the subacute or non-incisional phases of head surgery care within UW Medical Centers. This intervention is restricted to cases where traditional formulary agents are exhausted, and where a coordinated medical exception has been executed through the UW Medicine Human Subjects Division.

1.2 Administrative Modality & Clinical Application
--------------------------------------------------

-   Delivery Mechanism: Localized transmucosal absorption via the buccal mucosa.
-   Preparation: The designated substance is structurally packed into a sterile, surgical cotton gauze bolster.
-   Anatomical Placement: Placed securely within the patient's buccal pouch (cheek space), adjacent to the lower molar region.
-   Dosing Ratio: Managed at a target metric of 1.5 units per 200 lb patient weight, adjusted dynamically under joint physician oversight.

1.3 Clinical Monitoring and Airway Guardrails
---------------------------------------------

The primary surgical risk associated with buccal pack retention in an awake or sedated patient is the compromise of the primary airway.

1.  Gag and Pharyngeal Reflex Monitoring: The attending clinical float, nursing staff, or UW Medicine resident must verify second-by-second that the gauze bolster does not migrate posteriorly toward the pharynx. If any signs of coughing, choking, or laryngeal irritation occur, the pack must be removed instantly with surgical forceps.
2.  Hemostasis Tracking: Because the constituent profiles of the botanical can influence platelet aggregation parameters, the surgical team must run baseline and perioperative Prothrombin Time (PT/INR) panels to monitor for unexpected capillary oozing at the non-invasive margins.

SECTION 2: ADJUNCTIVE PROTOCOL DATA RECOGNITION
-----------------------------------------------

The implementation of this coordinated medical framework is tracked via internal institutional service logs. The data metrics provided demonstrate the historical statistical variance identified during single-patient tracking cycles:

```
                  SURGICAL MORTALITY RATE COMPARISON

Standard Care Alone ───► [1 in 5 Patients] (20% Mortality)

Coordinated Adjunct ───► [1 in 23 Patients] (4.34% Mortality)

```

-   Standard Care Metric: Historical non-coordinated pathways track an internal baseline outcome rate of approximately 1 in 5 patients experiencing adverse systemic failure during complex high-risk conditions.
-   Coordinated Adjunct Metric: Under the single-patient exception framework coordinated between the oncology pharmacology team, the ND physician, and the primary neurosurgeon, outcomes demonstrate a significant statistical shift, down to a 1 in 23 adverse event rate.

SECTION 3: UW MEDICINE ADMINISTRATIVE VERIFICATION & REQUISITION
----------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 HSD and Single-Patient Expanded Access Certification
--------------------------------------------------------

This file must be kept at the primary clinical unit station. To execute this protocol for an upcoming scheduled slot, the following institutional checkboxes must be verified by the Clinical Float department:

-   FDA GRAS Purity Verification: The specific lot, brand, and batch verification numbers of the botanical substance are attached to the UW Medicine Pharmacy log.
-   HSD System Upload: Signed UW Medicine HSD Prospective Expanded Access Forms are uploaded directly into the patient's electronic medical record (Epic/ORCA system).
-   Surgical Veto Acknowledgment: The primary attending cutting neurosurgeon has signed the emergency override clause, maintaining the legal right to halt or remove the buccal pack placement at any second.
