## The Institutional Review Board (IRB) Hospital Framework
When a novel or non-standard protocol is integrated into a surgical care plan, it must be governed by an Institutional Review Board (IRB) Compassionate Use Protocol or an Expanded Access Program (EAP) [1.1]. This administrative pathway allows licensed physicians to use investigational or non-formulary substances outside of traditional standard-of-care frameworks under strict ethical and legal boundaries [1.1].

## 1. Ethical Governance and Protocol Sanctioning
An IRB functions as an independent administrative body that reviews, monitors, and approves medical research and non-standard clinical interventions involving human subjects [1.1].

* The Objective: To validate that the patient's rights, safety, and informed consent are fully protected, ensuring that the potential therapeutic benefits of the non-standard protocol outweigh the inherent risks.
* The Constraint: The IRB does not change federal FDA drug laws. Instead, it creates an institutional legal shield that allows the specific clinical team to coordinate the treatment as an authorized, single-facility exception.

## 2. Risk Mitigation and Surgical Malpractice Controls
The protocol requires an extensive Informed Consent Form (ICF) and an Assumption of Risk Agreement signed by the patient, the integrative physician, and the neurosurgeon.

* Surgical Jurisdiction: The primary attending neurosurgeon maintains absolute clinical veto power over the entire procedure.
* Malpractice Indemnification: If any clinical metric—such as platelet aggregation or airway stability—deviates from baseline, the neurosurgeon reserves the unilateral authority to halt the integrative protocol immediately, return to standard emergency protocols, and report the event to protect patient safety.
