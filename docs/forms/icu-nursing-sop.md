# ICU Nursing Administration Record & Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Document Type:** Clinical Nursing Protocol & Verification Log  
**Protocol Reference:** IRB Alhalba-NB Neurosurgical Protocol (T. foenum-graecum)  
**Setting:** Intensive Care Unit (ICU) / Neurovascular Step-down

---

### 1. Pre-Administration Safety Verification (The "Double-Check" Rule)
Due to the concentrated nature of Alhalba-NB formulations, two licensed nurses (RNs) must independently verify the order against the patient’s active chart parameters before administration.

*   **Step 1:** Confirm the active order tier matches the patient's current clinical assignment.
*   **Step 2:** Verify that a Fingerstick Blood Glucose (FSBG) test has been performed within the last 2 hours.
*   **Step 3:** Confirm that the patient’s most recent PT/INR is < 1.5 and Serum Potassium is > 3.5 mEq/L.

### 2. Administration Instructions
*   **Route:** Oral (PO) or via Nasogastric / Orogastric (NG/OG) tube.
*   **NG/OG Tube Flush Protocol:** 
    1. Flush the tube with 30 mL of sterile water before administration.
    2. Administer the completely dissolved suspension immediately to prevent precipitate settling.
    3. Flush with an additional 30 mL of sterile water to guarantee full dose delivery and maintain tube patency.
*   **Timing:** Administer with or immediately following a scheduled enteral feeding or meal to mitigate potential gastrointestinal irritation and offset sudden insulinotropic shifts.

### 3. Post-Administration Monitoring Schedule
*   **Vital Signs:** Monitor Heart Rate and Blood Pressure every 1 hour for the first 4 hours post-induction dose (Tier 1), then every 4 hours during maintenance.
*   **Hypoglycemia Watch:** Check FSBG 2 hours post-dose. Implement the institutional hypoglycemia protocol immediately if blood glucose drops below 70 mg/dL.
*   **Hypersensitivity Monitoring:** Assess continuously for signs of an allergic reaction (e.g., localized rash, wheezing, periorbital edema). *Trigonella* shares high cross-reactivity with chickpea/legume allergens.
