### **CLINICAL NOTICE TO EVALUATING PHYSICIAN**
This screening tool must be completed by the attending Integrative Medicine physician or Neurosurgeon *prior* to the patient signing the Financial Liability Form and initiating Tier 1 or Tier 2 protocols. Concentrated *Trigonella foenum-graecum* (Alhalba-NB) contains highly potent, bioactive alkaloids (e.g., trigonelline), saponins, and 4-hydroxyisoleucine. These compounds present serious, documented drug-supplement interactions, particularly regarding hemostasis, glycemic control, and hepatic clearance.

### 2. High-Risk Drug Interaction Screening

Review the patient’s active Electronic Health Record (EHR) medication profile. Check all that apply:

#### 🔴 Section A: Anticoagulants & Antiplatelets (Absolute Contraindication Thresholds)
*Trigonella foenum-graecum* contains natural coumarin derivatives and saponins that inhibit platelet aggregation, compounding the risk of intracranial hemorrhage.
*   [  ] **Warfarin (Coumadin):** Active therapy. (Discontinue protocol if PT/INR > 1.5).
*   [  ] **Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs):** Apixaban (Eliquis), Rivaroxaban (Xarelto), Dabigatran (Pradaxa).
*   [  ] **Antiplatelet Therapy:** Clopidogrel (Plavix), Prasugrel (Effient), Ticagrelor (Brilinta), High-dose Aspirin (>81mg).
*   *Clinical Action:* If any box in Section A is checked, the protocol **cannot** proceed unless the neurosurgical team confirms these agents have been fully held and cleared according to standard pre-operative windows.

#### 🟡 Section B: Antidiabetic & Hypoglycemic Agents (High Monitoring Required)
The amino acid 4-hydroxyisoleucine acts as a potent insulin secretagogue. Combining it with conventional diabetes medications significantly elevates the risk of severe peri-operative hypoglycemia.
*   [  ] **Insulin Therapy:** Basal, bolus, or sliding-scale regimens.
*   [  ] **Sulfonylureas / Meglitinides:** Glipizide, Glimepiride, Glyburide, Repaglinide.
*   [  ] **Other Antidiabetic Agents:** Metformin, SGLT2 inhibitors (Jardiance), GLP-1 agonists (Ozempic).
*   *Clinical Action:* If any box in Section B is checked, initiate mandatory Fingerstick Blood Glucose (FSBG) monitoring every 4 to 6 hours upon protocol launch.

#### 🟡 Section C: Conventional Osmotic & Diuretic Therapies (Electrolyte Shift Risk)
Co-administration with standard neuro-osmotic agents can accelerate hypokalemia.
*   [  ] **Loop Diuretics:** Furosemide (Lasix), Bumetanide.
*   [  ] **Osmotic Diuretics:** Mannitol, Hypertonic Saline (3% or 23.4% continuous infusions).
*   *Clinical Action:* Require comprehensive metabolic panels (CMP) every 12 to 24 hours to track potassium and sodium fluctuations.

### 4. Clinical Contraindications & Co-Morbidities
Check if the patient has a documented history of any of the following conditions:
*   [  ] **Severe Chickpea / Legume Allergy:** Cross-reactivity with *Trigonella* proteins is highly prevalent and can trigger systemic anaphylaxis.
*   [  ] **Active, Uncontrolled Systemic Bleeding Diathesis** (e.g., Hemophilia, Von Willebrand disease).
*   [  ] **History of Refractory Hypoglycemia Unrelated to Diabetes.**
