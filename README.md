# Integrative Neurosurgical Edema Management Protocol (Alhalba-NB)

This repository serves as the definitive clinical, financial, and regulatory source of truth for the adjunct integration of optimized *Trigonella foenum-graecum* (Alhalba-NB) formulations. Operating under an Institutional Review Board (IRB) approved Single-Patient Expanded Access / Compassionate Use framework, this protocol targets the measured reduction of vasogenic and cytotoxic peritumoral or post-traumatic brain swelling.

---

## 1. Field Definition: Integrative Neuro-Oncology & Neuro-Trauma
Traditional neurosurgical edema control relies almost exclusively on aggressive osmotic therapies (e.g., Mannitol, Hypertonic Saline) and high-dose synthetic corticosteroids (Dexamethasone). While effective, these therapies carry profound systemic toxicities, including severe steroid-induced hyperglycemia, muscle wasting, and profound fluid shifts. 

This protocol establishes a validation pipeline for **Integrative Neurosurgery**. By introducing highly concentrated, bioavailable phyto-alkaloids and secretagogues, we target parallel secondary injury pathways—specifically closing astrocytic water channels and stabilizing endothelial basement membranes—without introducing additional systemic metabolic failure.

---

## 2. Molecular Targets in Neurosurgery
The operational phases of this repository track explicit, quantifiable neuro-radiological and chemical metrics:
*   **Capillary Endothelial Protection:** Trigonelline blocks Matrix Metalloproteinase-9 (MMP-9) upregulation, preserving tight junctions (Claudin-5/Occludin) to arrest vasogenic fluid leakage into the brain parenchyma.
*   **Astrocytic Water Channel Regulation:** Target compounds downregulate Aquaporin-4 (AQP4) expressions on astrocytic foot-processes, reducing intracellular fluid accumulation (cytotoxic edema).
*   **Steroid-Sparing Glycemic Control:** The inclusion of 4-hydroxyisoleucine acts as a glucose-dependent insulin secretagogue, combating the severe insulin resistance secondary to peri-operative dexamethasone use.

---

## 3. Take-Home Maintenance: Outpatient Expanded Access Pathway
When a patient achieves clinical stability and transitions out of acute inpatient neurosurgical settings, the inflammatory cascade must still be continuously suppressed to prevent rebound edema during the steroid taper. 

Under **FDA Single-Patient Expanded Access (21 CFR § 312.310)**, the patient is transitioned to a self-administered, out-of-pocket maintenance tier (Alhalba-NB-1350mg) at home. Because the investigational product leaves the highly controlled, monitored environment of the neuro-ICU, strict regulatory documentation, chain of custody, and outpatient safety reporting sheets must be maintained.
